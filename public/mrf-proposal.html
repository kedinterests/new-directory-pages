The Opportunity

MRF has outgrown its design

Mineral Rights Forum is the most authoritative community for mineral owners in the country. The design hasn't kept pace with the reputation.

First impressions matter

A new mineral rights owner lands on MRF from Google. Within three seconds they judge whether this is a trustworthy source or an outdated one. A modern design answers that question before they read a word.

The MRF county directories — already live — have a polished, professional identity: dark navy, gold accents, Playfair Display. The forum should feel like it belongs to the same family, not a different product entirely.

Zero-Risk Transition

Nobody gets left behind

The biggest risk with any redesign is alienating users who are comfortable with what they know. This plan eliminates that risk completely with a three-phase approach that keeps every member in control of their own experience.

Discourse is built for this. Simultaneous themes, user theme-switching, and preview modes are all supported, documented features of the Discourse platform — not hacks, not workarounds. Hundreds of large Discourse communities have done this exact rollout.

1
Phase 1 — Months 1–3

Opt-in: "Try the new design"

The current design stays exactly as it is. A small, tasteful banner appears at the top of the forum: "The new Mineral Rights Forum is here — try it now." Members who click are taken to the new design. Members who ignore it see nothing different. The new design runs alongside the old one; Discourse handles both simultaneously at zero performance cost.

Members who try it and don't like it can switch back in one click. Their preference is saved to their account. No pressure, no commitment.

No member is ever forced to change
2
Phase 2 — Months 4–6

Soft switch: New design becomes default

After 3 months of opt-in data — you'll be able to see exactly how many members chose the new design, how their session times and post rates compare, and whether there's any negative signal — the new design becomes the default for all visitors and new sign-ups.

Members who have been using the old design keep it automatically. A quiet "Back to classic view" link in the footer gives anyone else the option to revert. The old design is still fully live.

You control the timing — no fixed deadline
3
Phase 3 — Month 7+

Migration complete: One design, one brand

By this point, usage data will tell you what percentage of members are still on the old design. In practice, across communities that have done this rollout, that number drops below 5% within 4–6 months. When you and Kenny are comfortable, the old design is retired.

There's no pressure on timing. If the data shows members need more time, Phase 2 extends. If adoption is fast, Phase 3 can come sooner. The data leads.

You decide when — the data backs the decision

At every phase, you can pause or reverse

If at any point in Phase 1 you see members complaining, engagement dropping, or any other negative signal — you remove the banner and the new design is simply gone. Zero members were ever forced onto it. There is no "breaking point" in this rollout. It is entirely reversible at every step until Phase 3.

Under the Hood

What Discourse supports natively

This isn't a gamble on untested technology. Discourse's theme system is mature, documented, and used by thousands of communities for exactly this kind of rollout.

Built-in — color scheme editor
  • Navy header background
  • Gold for links, buttons, highlights
  • Full light/dark mode support
  • Takes 5 minutes in Discourse admin
Custom CSS — light work
  • Playfair Display + Inter fonts (Google Fonts import)
  • Gold border on header
  • Topic list hover states and tag pill styling
  • Active nav pill gold underline
Theme components — HTML/CSS
  • Hero banner below the header
  • "Try the new design" opt-in banner
  • Directory promo card in sidebar
  • Custom footer with brand treatment
Not possible — Discourse limits
  • Restructuring the topic list columns
  • Changing the fundamental page layout
  • Running two separate Discourse instances
  • Any change to post content or data
